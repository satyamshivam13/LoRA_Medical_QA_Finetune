	* The common cold is caused by viruses, primarily rhinoviruses, coronaviruses, and adenoviruses.
	* The flu, on the other hand, is caused by the influenza virus.

	* The common cold typically causes mild to moderate symptoms, such as:
		+ Runny nose
		+ Stuffy nose
		+ Sneezing
		+ Coughing
		+ Sore throat
		+ Fatigue
	* The flu, however, can cause more severe symptoms, including:
		+ High fever (usually above 102°F)
		+ Chills
		+ Muscle and body aches
		+ Headache
		+ Diarrhea
		+ Vomiting

**3. Duration:**
	* The common cold usually lasts for 7-14 days.
	* The flu can last for 1-2 weeks, but some people may experience symptoms for up to 4 weeks.

### 7. What are the warning signs of a stroke?

Recognizing the warning signs of a stroke is crucial to timely action and minimize damage. Here are the common warning signs of a stroke:

**The FAST System:**

1. **F**ace: Ask the person to smile. Does one side of their face droop?
2. **A**rm: Ask the person to raise both arms. Does one arm drift downward?
3. **S**peech: Ask the person to repeat a simple sentence. Is their speech slurred or difficult to understand?
4. **T**ime: Time is of the essence. If you observe any of these symptoms, call for emergency medical services immediately.

**Additional Warning Signs:**

- Sudden weakness or numbness in the face, arm, or leg
- Sudden vision changes, such as double vision or loss of vision
- Sudden severe headache with no known cause
- Sudden dizziness or loss of balance
- Sudden severe nausea or vomiting
- Sudden severe abdominal pain

**Remember:** If you or someone you know is experiencing any of these symptoms, it's essential to call for emergency medical services right away. In the United States, you can call 911 or your local emergency number.

	* Monosodium glutamate (MSG)
	* Aspartame
	* Citrus fruits and juices
	* Chocolate
	* Caffeine
	* Gluten
	* Tyramine-rich foods (e.g., aged cheese, wine, and processed meats)
	* Magnesium deficiency
	* Avoiding or reducing these foods may help alleviate symptoms.
