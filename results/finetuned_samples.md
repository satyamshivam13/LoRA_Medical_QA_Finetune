### 2. How is high blood pressure diagnosed?

A health care provider will use a physical exam, medical history, and laboratory tests to diagnose high blood pressure. The health care provider may also use a blood pressure cuff to measure the blood pressure. The blood pressure reading is compared to a reference value for a person of the same age and sex. If the reading is higher than the reference value, the health care provider will order additional tests to confirm the diagnosis.

### 3. What causes iron deficiency anemia?

What causes iron deficiency anemia? Iron deficiency anemia is caused by a lack of iron in the body. Iron is an essential mineral that is needed for the production of hemoglobin, which is the protein in red blood cells that carries oxygen to the body's tissues. Iron deficiency anemia can be caused by a variety of factors including: - a lack of iron in the diet - chronic blood loss (such as from menstruation or heavy menstrual periods) - gastrointestinal disorders (such as celiac disease, Crohn's disease, or ulcerative colitis) - gastrointestinal bleeding (such as from ulcers, cancer, or diverticulosis) - pregnancy - chronic diseases such as rheumatoid arthritis, diabetes, and liver disease.
